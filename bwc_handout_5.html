Roades' Field Notes

There is a cave in the hills that Roades considers to be of great archaeological significance. The notes contain a sketch map that shows the cave's location. As the notes progress, he starts referring to it as a holy site.

Roades has had discussions with Sheriff Sprouston, who also believes that the cave is holy. Roades does not trust Sprouston's motivations, though.

His wife, Abigail, has had an accident at the cave and is trapped there.

In the later notes, Roades occasionally refers to Abigail as his mother.

One note mentions that Abigail is the source of the newly revived creek.

Roades believes the water has special properties. In the later part of the journal, he refers to it repeatedly as "The Water of Life".

In the last pages, Roades talks about how the voice of his mother, Abigail, is in his head conscantly now. He seems alternately comforted and terrified by this.
